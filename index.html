In the last two decades, scientists have discovered more and more about how the heart works—its physiology; how it impacts other parts of the body—its conductivity; and how it regulates our emotions—its psychophysiology. In all, our knowledge of how it contributes to our whole health continues to expand.

You may already know that the heart pumps blood, oxygen, and nutrients throughout the body. This job is essential to life. But the heart is responsible for far more than circulation alone. Scientists now understand that the heart also plays a vital role in how we feel, how we respond to stress, and how well we think and focus.

Over the next two to three weeks, we will explore the heart beyond its muscular function. We will learn how it communicates with the brain, how it helps us build resilience in the face of stress, and how heart-based skills can improve our overall health and well-being.

Learning Segment 1

The Heart Is More Than a Pump

The Heart as a Communication Center.
For a long time, scientists believed the heart’s main job was mechanical: pumping blood through the body. While this job is essential, modern research shows that the heart is also a powerful communication center.

The heart constantly sends messages to the brain. In fact, it sends more information to the brain than the brain sends to the heart. These messages help shape how we feel, how we think, and how we respond to the world around us.

This means your heart is not just reacting to your thoughts, it is actively helping guide your experiences.

Heart–Brain Communication

tap to flip

The constant exchange of information between the heart and the brain affects how we feel, think, and respond to stress. The heart helps guide the brain, not just the other way around.

⚡

Nervous System

tap to flip

The body’s message system, made of the brain, spinal cord, and nerves, sends fast signals to control movement, thoughts, and emotions.

🧪

Hormones

tap to flip

Chemical messengers that travel through the bloodstream and change how the body feels and functions, including stress and mood.

↔️

Two-Way Signaling

tap to flip

Communication that goes both directions between the heart and brain. Each one sends messages that influence the other.
